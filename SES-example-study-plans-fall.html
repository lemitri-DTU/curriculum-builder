Example Study Plans — MSc Sustainable Energy Systems (Fall Start)

DTU 2025 Curriculum · 10 student profiles · Thesis (30 ECTS) assumed in Spring of Year 2 unless noted

These ten example plans show how different student profiles might structure their 120 ECTS MSc programme (90 ECTS courses + 30 ECTS thesis) when starting in Fall. Each plan satisfies all pool requirements for its chosen specialization. Polytechnic foundation courses and Innovation II courses are included. Where noted, 5 ECTS may be scheduled alongside the thesis. The Study Plan Builder tool lets you replicate and customize these plans interactively. Course colors match the requirement pools shown in the builder for each specialization.
1
The AI & Data Engineer
Digital Energy Systems
Builds the full data-to-deployment pipeline for energy AI. Computational Tools for Data Science and ML for Energy Systems form the digital core in Fall Y1, with Scientific Programming for hands-on coding skills in Spring. MLOps in January bridges models to production. Year 2 stacks Deep Learning, Intelligent Systems (IoT and distributed AI), and Applied AI for Entrepreneurs — connecting technical depth with the ability to prototype and ship AI-powered energy products.

The Energy Economist
Energy Systems Analysis
Markets, policy, and the economics of the energy transition. Life Cycle Assessment (10 ECTS) anchors the sustainability analysis, paired with Energy Economics, Environmental & Resource Economics, and Decision Support for strategic assessment. Integer Programming and Dynamic Optimization provide the quantitative backbone for modelling energy markets and investment decisions. Wind Farm Planning and Large Scale Energy System Analysis ground the economics in real project contexts.

The Power Systems Engineer
General
The complete power systems toolkit, from fundamentals to advanced control. Electric Power Systems (10 ECTS) and Stability & Control in Fall Y1 build a rigorous grid dynamics foundation. Spring covers Power Grid Analysis, Balancing with Large Scale Wind, and PV Systems for generation-side understanding. Year 2 adds Model Predictive Control for real-time grid operation and ML for Energy Systems for data-driven forecasting — the modern power engineer's dual toolkit.

The Energy Systems Generalist
General
The broadest tour of the energy landscape — ideal for students who want to keep career doors open. Covers storage, PV, and district heating across the technology spectrum; Energy Economics and Technology Entrepreneurship for the business angle; Climate Change physics and Physics of Sustainable Energy for scientific grounding. Decision Support and Large Scale Energy System Analysis tie it together at the systems level. A strong foundation for energy consulting, policy, or further specialization.

The Wind Energy Specialist
General
All-in on wind, from turbine to transmission. Wind Turbine Technology (10 ECTS) in Fall Y1 delivers the aerodynamic and mechanical foundations, while Physics of Sustainable Energy and Offshore Wind HVDC provide the physical and grid-connection context. Wind Farm Planning in January adds the project development angle. Spring covers Scientific Programming for Wind, Grid Analysis, and Power System Balancing. Year 2 completes the picture with Probabilistic Methods, ML for Energy Systems, and Wind Power Integration.

The Hydrogen & Power-to-X Pioneer
General
The full Power-to-X value chain, from electrons to molecules. Energy Storage & Conversion in Fall Y1 grounds the electrochemistry, while Digitalization of Thermal Energy Technologies adds the simulation toolkit. Spring Y1 covers both ends of sector coupling: Green Fuels & P2X for the hydrogen pathway and Sustainable District Heating for the heat sector. Energy & Sustainability and Physics of Sustainable Energy in Fall Y2 provide the broader transition context. Hydrogen & Fuel Cells alongside the thesis enables deep research into the technology that ties it all together.

The Cleantech Entrepreneur
General

Risk Management in January ties it together.

General
Where power systems meet IoT and embedded intelligence. Electric Power Systems (10 ECTS) and Intelligent Systems run in parallel in Fall Y1 — grid fundamentals alongside distributed AI, knowledge representation, and smart-grid architectures. The Intelligent Systems project in January Y2 turns theory into a working prototype. Model Predictive Control handles real-time grid operation, while Networked Embedded Systems in Fall Y2 adds the hardware layer. Energy Storage completes the picture with flexibility technologies.

The Sustainability & Societal Challenges Expert
General
The most interdisciplinary path — bridging engineering, social science, and design for sustainability. History of Technology grounds the energy transition in how past technological shifts reshaped society. Socio-technical Dimensions of Renewable Energy and Environmental Management & Ethics explore the human and governance sides. System Sustainability Tools provides hands-on assessment methods. The capstone: Holistic Design of Engineering Systems (10 ECTS) alongside the thesis, designing socio-technical solutions with real stakeholders — complemented by Social Data Analysis for quantitative insight into societal dynamics.
